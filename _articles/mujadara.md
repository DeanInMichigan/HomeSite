## Ingredients

- ½ cup olive oil
- 2 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
- 4½ cups water
- 2 cups brown lentils, rinsed and drained
- Salt
- 1 cup long grain white rice, rinsed
- 2 teaspoons cumin

## Instructions

1. In a medium bowl, cover the rice with cold water. Set aside.
2. Line a plate with paper towels. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over high heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring constantly, until deeply browned and starting to crisp around the edges, about 20 minutes. Transfer to the plate and set aside — they will continue to crisp as they cool.
3. Add the lentils and a generous pinch of salt to another pot with the water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and cook for 15 minutes.
4. Drain the soaked rice, rinse, and transfer to the pot with the lentils. Stir once, cover tightly, and cook undisturbed for 15 minutes.
5. Remove from heat. Rest, covered, for 5 minutes. Add the cumin and fluff the rice and lentils with a fork.
6. Spoon the crispy onions over the top. Serve warm, with cucumber yogurt sauce if desired.

## Notes

- **Storage:** Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the fridge for 4–5 days.
- **Freezing:** Freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and microwave to reheat.
- **Make ahead:** Caramelize the onions up to a day in advance to reduce prep time.
- **Substitutes:** Green lentils work in place of brown; brown rice or bulgur can replace the white rice.
