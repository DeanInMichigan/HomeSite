## Ingredients

- 2 stale pitta or other flatbreads
- 1 garlic clove
- Juice of ½ lemon
- ½ tablespoon cider vinegar
- 3 tablespoons good olive oil, plus extra to grease
- 1 teaspoon sumac, plus extra to sprinkle
- 500g ripe mixed tomatoes
- 3 small cucumbers or 1 large one
- 2 spring onions
- 25g flat-leaf parsley
- 15g mint
- 100g purslane (optional)
- 100g radishes

## Instructions

1. Heat the oven to 200°C. Toss the pitta with a little olive oil, then bake for about 15 minutes until crisp. Allow to cool slightly, then break into shards.
2. Meanwhile, crush the garlic clove and put in a jar with the lemon juice, vinegar, oil, and sumac. Season and shake well, then leave to infuse until the salad is ready.
3. Cut the tomatoes into irregular chunks and the cucumber into rough 1.5cm dice (deseed if using the large variety). Finely slice the spring onions and roughly chop the herb leaves. Pick purslane leaves but leave whole. Combine in a large bowl.
4. Toss the dressing with the salad. Just before serving, quarter the radishes and use them to top the salad, along with the pita shards and a final flourish of sumac.
