Easy, delicious, decadent rice pudding with a caramel-pecan topping. One bite and you'll be transported to another plane of existence. Prep: 5 min · Cook: 40 min · Serves: 8.

## Ingredients

**Rice pudding:**

- ½ cup raisins (golden or regular)
- ½ cup bourbon or other whiskey (optional)
- 1 cup medium grain white rice
- 2 cups water
- 2 cups milk (1% works well)
- 2 tablespoons heavy cream
- 1 tablespoon salted butter
- Pinch of salt
- 8 oz sweetened condensed milk, more if needed
- Dash of ground cinnamon
- Dash of ground nutmeg
- 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
- 1 egg, beaten

**Caramel-pecan sauce:**

- ½ cup butter
- ½ cup packed brown sugar
- ½ cup light corn syrup
- ½ cup chopped pecans
- 1 tablespoon bourbon or other whiskey (optional)

## Instructions

1. Combine the raisins and whiskey in a small bowl. Set aside for 1 hour.
2. In a medium nonstick saucepan, combine the rice, water, milk, cream, butter, and salt. Bring to a gentle boil, then cover, reduce to low, and simmer for 20–25 minutes, stirring twice. The rice should be cooked but there should still be visible creamy liquid — not all absorbed.
3. Remove from heat and add the sweetened condensed milk, cinnamon, nutmeg, and vanilla. Return to low heat for 5 minutes.
4. Remove from heat and slowly drizzle in the beaten egg, stirring constantly. Drain the raisins and stir them in. Add more condensed milk if you'd like it creamier, or return to the stovetop for 3–4 minutes if it's too loose.
5. **Caramel-pecan sauce:** While the rice cooks in step 2, combine the butter, brown sugar, corn syrup, and pecans in a small saucepan. Heat over medium-low, then let bubble gently for 5 minutes until it reaches a caramel consistency. Remove from heat and stir in the bourbon if using.
6. Serve the rice pudding in small bowls topped with caramel-pecan sauce.
