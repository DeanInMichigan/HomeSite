This Birria Tacos recipe is juicy, cheesy, delicious — a true showstopper. It begins with braising a birria de rez and then making a consomé and frying up tacos with Oaxacan cheese.

## Ingredients

**Birria de Rez:**

- 2 pounds boneless chuck
- 1 pound oxtail or bone-in short ribs
- 1 teaspoon neutral oil (avocado or vegetable oil)

**Sauce:**

- 7 ancho chiles (ends trimmed and de-seeded)
- 7 guajillo chiles (ends trimmed and de-seeded)
- 3 chiles de arbol (ends trimmed and de-seeded)
- 1 white onion (peeled and halved)
- 6 garlic cloves (peeled)
- 4 roma tomatoes
- 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
- 1 teaspoon dried Mexican oregano
- 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
- 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
- ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
- ½ Mexican cinnamon stick
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
- 3 cups beef broth or water (divided)

**Tacos:**

- ¼ cup minced cilantro
- ¼ white onion (minced)
- Juice from 1 lime
- Kosher salt
- Corn tortillas
- 3 ounces Oaxacan cheese (or mozzarella)

## Instructions

**Sear the meat:**

1. Bring the meat to room temperature, about 30 minutes, then sprinkle liberally on all sides with kosher salt. In a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat, add the oil. When hot, sear the meat on all sides until browned — do this in batches. Transfer to a bowl.

**Make the sauce:**

2. In another pot, add the dried chiles, halved onion, garlic, tomatoes, spices, and bay leaves. Cover with cold water and simmer over medium heat for about 15 minutes. Strain and transfer the solids to a blender.
3. Add the apple cider vinegar and about 1 cup of broth. Blend until very smooth, about 2 minutes. Add salt to taste. *For a smoother sauce, strain through a fine-mesh strainer.*

**Braise the meat:**

4. Preheat the oven to 300°F. Return the meat to the pot and pour the sauce over it. Add the remaining 2 cups of broth to the blender, swish around to capture leftover sauce, and pour into the pot. Bring to a gentle simmer, then cover and transfer to the oven. Cook for about 3 hours, until the meat is tender.

**Assemble the tacos:**

5. Mix together the cilantro, white onion, lime juice, and salt.
6. Remove the meat from the sauce and shred with two forks. Ladle the broth into a bowl and add a handful of diced cilantro.
7. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat. Dip a tortilla into the fat at the top of the broth and place it in the skillet. Pan-fry for about 30 seconds, then flip. Add shredded meat and cheese, fold over, and cook until golden on both sides, about 1 minute. Serve alongside the broth for dipping.
