Shirazi salad is a classic Persian side dish — crisp cucumbers, juicy tomatoes, fresh herbs, and a punchy lemon-sumac dressing. It comes together in minutes but gets even better after a short rest in the fridge.

## Ingredients

### Salad

- 4 mini cucumbers (Persian cucumbers), small diced
- 10 oz. grape tomatoes, small diced (heirloom or any variety)
- ½ cup red onions, minced
- ¼ cup fresh mint, chopped
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
- Zest of 1 lemon (save the juice for the dressing)

### Dressing

- Juice of 2 medium lemons (¼ cup)
- 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tablespoon dried dill weed
- 1 teaspoon sumac
- Salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions

1. Wash and chop all vegetables and herbs. Dice the cucumbers and tomatoes small, mince the red onion, and finely chop the parsley, mint, and cilantro.
2. Zest one lemon. (You can zest both and freeze the extra zest for another recipe.)
3. Juice both lemons — you need about ¼ cup of juice.
4. Add the cucumbers, tomatoes, red onion, mint, parsley, cilantro, and lemon zest to a mixing bowl. Stir to distribute evenly.
5. In a small measuring cup, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, dried dill, sumac, salt, and pepper.
6. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to coat.
7. Serve immediately, or refrigerate for 30 minutes to let the flavors meld — it's worth the wait.
