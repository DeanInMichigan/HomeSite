A two-pound batch makes about 15 kafta.

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs ground meat (lamb, beef, or a mix)
- 1¼ cup onions, finely minced
- 8 cloves garlic
- ⅔ cup fresh mint (about 7½ tablespoons)
- 1¼ cup fresh parsley
- 2½ teaspoons cumin
- 1¼ teaspoons coriander
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon pepper

## Instructions

1. Combine all ingredients thoroughly in a large bowl.

**Traditional (baked):** With wet fingers, roll into balls about 1½ oz each (or use a ¼-cup measure). Bake on a sheet pan at 375°F for 10–15 minutes.

**Meat loaf version:** Place mixture in a loaf pan and cook at 400°F for 40 minutes.

**Grill version:** Thread a skewer through each meatball and mold into a long finger shape along the skewer. Grill or broil gently, being careful not to overcook so it stays moist. Remove from skewers with Lebanese pita.

## Notes

Serve with tomatoes, lettuce, yogurt, and soft pita bread. To eat: cut a piece of pita and wrap it around the kafta like a blanket. Add tomatoes, cucumber, lettuce, and a generous spoonful of plain yogurt.
