This Mediterranean cheesy mashed potato casserole is amazing — flavored with tangy feta, sharp pecorino romano, and Mediterranean herbs. Prep: 20 min · Cook: 1 hour · Serves: 8.

## Ingredients

- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 medium onion
- 2¾ lbs (1¼ kg) potatoes
- 2 eggs
- ¾ cup grated pecorino romano or graviera
- ¼ cup crumbled feta
- 1 tablespoon dry oregano
- ¾ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon freshly ground pepper
- ¼ cup chopped parsley
- 2 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped
- 2 tablespoons olive oil

## Instructions

1. In a medium pan over medium heat, warm 1 tablespoon olive oil. Sauté the onion until soft, about 5–7 minutes. Set aside.
2. Place whole, unpeeled potatoes in a pot. Add cold water to cover by about an inch and a bit of salt. Boil until pierced easily with a fork, about 20 minutes. Drain, run cold water over them, and peel once cool enough to handle.
3. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
4. Mash the potatoes with a potato masher or food mill and place in a large bowl.
5. Lightly beat the eggs and add to the potatoes. Add the onion, cheese, herbs, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
6. Coat an 8×12 inch baking dish with olive oil. Spread the potato mixture in evenly. Make triangle patterns with a fork.
7. Drizzle 2 tablespoons of olive oil over the casserole.
8. Bake until golden, about 50–60 minutes. Let cool for at least 30 minutes before slicing.

## Notes

- **Make ahead:** Assemble a day ahead, refrigerate, then bring to room temperature for 30 minutes before baking at 400°F for 50–60 minutes.
- **Storage:** Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for 3–4 days. Freeze cut squares for up to 4 months.
- **Reheat:** Bake individual portions at 350°F for 35–50 minutes depending on whether frozen or thawed.
