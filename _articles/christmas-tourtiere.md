For such a simple meat-filled pie, tourtière certainly has a vast number of different incarnations. As one French baker says, "There are as many recipes for tourtière as there are cooks in Quebec." Woe betide the Québécois who misses their tourtière at Christmas! Prep: 35–50 min · Bake: 45 min.

## Ingredients

**Crust:**

- 2½ cups Pastry Flour Blend or all-purpose flour
- 10 tablespoons cold butter
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 5–8 tablespoons ice water

**Filling:**

- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 cups water
- 1 large potato (about 12 oz), peeled and cut into ½-inch dice
- 2 pounds ground pork (or a mix of pork and beef)
- 1 medium-to-large onion, diced
- 1–2 large cloves garlic, finely chopped
- ½ teaspoon ground clove
- ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
- ¼ teaspoon ground allspice
- ¾ teaspoon ground sage
- ½ teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- ½–¾ teaspoon salt, to taste

## Instructions

1. **Make the filling:** Put the salt, water, and potato in a medium saucepan and bring to a boil. Cook until fork-tender, about 5–6 minutes. Drain, saving the water. Mash about half the potatoes, leaving the rest in chunks.
2. In a large skillet, brown the meat, draining off any excess fat.
3. Add the onion, garlic, spices, salt, and reserved potato water to the meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 35 minutes, until the liquid has evaporated and the onions are tender.
4. Stir in the mashed potatoes until thoroughly combined. Gently stir in the diced potatoes. Set aside to cool.
5. **Make the crust:** Combine flour, salt, and baking powder in a bowl. Add the butter and mix in thoroughly — unlike a typical American pie crust, this short crust should have no large pieces of butter; the mixture should look like breadcrumbs.
6. Drizzle in the water, tossing as you go, until the dough is cohesive. Divide into two pieces (one slightly larger). Shape each into a flattened disk and wrap in plastic. Refrigerate for 30 minutes.
7. Preheat oven to 375°F. Roll the larger piece of dough into a 13-inch circle and settle it into a 9-inch cast iron skillet or pie pan. Spoon the cooled filling in and pat flat.
8. Roll the remaining dough into a 10-inch circle and lay it on top. Tuck the overhanging bottom crust over the edge, pinching to seal. Crimp the edges and cut a vent hole in the center.
9. Bake for 45 minutes until golden brown. Let cool for 10–15 minutes before serving.

## Notes

- This pie freezes well before or after baking. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before baking or rewarming.
- Some cooks substitute chicken broth for the water in the filling — reduce the salt accordingly.
- Cinnamon, savory, rosemary, and diced celery are common additions to the filling spice blend.
