## Ingredients

- 1½ pounds stew meat
- Salt and pepper
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 10 baby bella mushrooms, quartered
- 3 cups mirepoix (onion, celery, and carrot, chopped)
- 6–8 cloves garlic, minced
- 6 cups low sodium beef broth (or vegetable)
- 1 cup water
- 2 bay leaves
- ½ teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 large potato, shredded
- ⅔ cup pearl barley, rinsed

## Instructions

1. Season the stew meat with a good pinch of salt and pepper. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pot or Instant Pot over medium-high heat. Add half the stew meat and brown on all sides, about 2–3 minutes total. Remove to a plate and repeat with the second batch.
2. Add the mushrooms to the pot and brown for 1–2 minutes, picking up the brown bits left behind by the meat. Remove to the same plate.
3. Add a little more oil if needed, then add the mirepoix. Cook for 4–5 minutes until onions soften. Add garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds.
4. **Slow Cooker:** Add the softened veggies, stew meat, mushrooms, bay leaves, thyme, water, and broth to the slow cooker. Cook on high for 3 hours or low for 6 hours.
5. **Instant Pot:** Add the stew meat, mushrooms, bay leaves, thyme, water, and broth to the sautéed veggies. Pressure cook on high for 13–16 minutes. Allow pressure to release naturally.
6. **Both methods:** Stir in the shredded potatoes and barley. Cook on high for 1 hour until potatoes and barley are tender. Season to taste. Serve warm with chopped parsley and crusty bread.

## Notes

- For a little heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes along with the dried thyme.
